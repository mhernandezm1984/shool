Introduction
What Is a Complex Machine?

⚙️ Simple Machine

A simple machine is a tool that makes work easier! It helps us push, pull or lift things with less effort. There are 6 kinds!

🔗 Complex Machine

A complex machine combines 2 or more simple machines as a team. Each one helps the next one!

  • More powerful than alone
  • Each part has a special job
  • Together = amazing things! 🚀
"This is a complex machine. It is made of many simple machines working together as a team!"

Simple Machine #1
🔧 The Lever

What is a Lever?

A lever is like a seesaw! It is a bar that turns on a special point called a fulcrum. Push one side down and the other goes up!

🚲 On the bicycle:

  • Handlebars — turn to steer left or right
  • Brake levers — squeeze to slow down
  • Pedal cranks — push to go forward!

A small push on the grip makes the big front wheel turn! 💪

"The handlebars are levers. They help us steer the bike easily with just a little bit of force."
🖱️ Hover to see it move!

The 3 parts: Bar (handlebar) + Fulcrum (pivot) + Output (wheel turns). Simple! ✅

Simple Machine #2
⭕ Wheel & Axle

What is it?

A big wheel connected to a small rod (axle). When the wheel spins, the axle spins too! It makes things move easier and smoother.

🚲 On the bicycle:

  • Both wheels — spin on the axle to roll forward
  • Pedal cranks — also a wheel & axle!
  • Less friction = faster, smoother riding!

Without wheels you'd have to drag the bike! That would be very hard! 😅

"The wheels and the axle help us roll fast! They reduce friction so the bike moves smoothly."
🖱️ Hover each part!

Also wheel & axle: Pedal axle, headset — every spinning part!

Simple Machine #3
⛓ The Pulley

What is a Pulley?

A pulley is a wheel with a rope or chain around it. Pull one end and the other side moves! It changes the direction of force to make lifting easier.

🚲 On the bicycle:

  • Chain + gears — the chain is like a pulley rope!
  • Chainring — big front gear connected to pedals
  • Rear cog — small gear on the back wheel

Your legs push the pedals → chain → rear wheel = you move! 🚀

"The chain and gears work like a pulley — they carry my leg power all the way to the back wheel!"
🖱️ Hover to see the chain move!

Fun fact: Flagpoles use a pulley too — you pull DOWN and the flag goes UP! 🏳️

Simple Machine #4
🔩 The Screw

What is a Screw?

A screw is like an inclined plane wrapped around a stick! When you turn it round and round, it moves forward and holds things very tightly together.

🚲 On the bicycle:

  • Bolts & nuts — hold wheels, seat & handlebars
  • Pedal screws — attach pedals to cranks
  • Brake screws — keep brakes in place

Without screws, the bike would fall apart in seconds! They are tiny superheroes! 🦸

"Screws are everywhere on the bike — they are tiny superheroes holding everything together!"
🖱️ Hover each screw!

Remember: Righty tighty = goes in. Lefty loosey = comes out! 😄

Simple Machine #5
🪓 The Wedge

What is a Wedge?

A wedge is like two ramps put back to back! It has a sharp, thin edge that splits things apart or holds them still. Think of a knife or an axe!

🚲 On the bicycle:

  • Brake pads — wedge shape squeezes the wheel to stop!
  • Tire tread — tiny wedges grip the road
  • Pedal teeth — grip your shoe so your foot doesn't slip

Squeeze the brake lever → pad presses wheel → friction stops you safely! 🛑

"The brake pads are wedges — when they squeeze the wheel, friction stops the bike!"
🖱️ Hover to squeeze the brakes!

How it works: More squeeze = more friction = stops faster! The wedge shape makes this happen! ✋

Simple Machine #6
📐 Inclined Plane

What is it?

An inclined plane is simply a RAMP! 🏔️ Instead of lifting something straight up (hard!), you push it up a slope — much easier! The longer the ramp, the less force you need!

🚲 On the bicycle:

  • Hills & ramps — the road slope is an inclined plane!
  • Frame angles — slanted frame absorbs bumps
  • Gear teeth shape — tiny inclined planes that catch the chain

A long gentle hill is easier than going straight up a wall — that's the inclined plane! ⛰️

"A ramp is an inclined plane — it lets us ride up hills without using too much energy!"
🖱️ Hover to see the bike climb!

Key rule: LONG gentle ramp = EASY. SHORT steep ramp = HARD. Same height, different effort!
