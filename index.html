⚙️ Simple Machine

A simple machine is a device with few or no moving parts that makes work easier. It changes the direction or size of a force.

🔗 Complex Machine

A complex machine combines two or more simple machines working as a team. Each one passes its force to the next!

  • More power than one simple machine alone
  • Each part does a different job
  • Together they accomplish amazing things!
"This is a complex machine. It is made of many simple machines working together as a team!"

🔧 What Is a Lever?

A lever is a rigid bar that pivots on a fulcrum. It lets you move a heavy load with a smaller force. There are 3 types of levers!

📐 Class 1 Lever — Handlebars

  • Input Force: Your hands pushing the handlebars
  • Fulcrum: The steering column pivot point
  • Output Force: The front wheel changes direction
"The handlebars are levers. They help us steer the bike easily with just a little bit of force."
🖱️ Hover each part to see it move!

More levers: Brake levers, pedal cranks, kickstand — levers everywhere!

⭕ What Is a Wheel & Axle?

A wheel and axle is a large wheel attached to a smaller rod (axle). When the wheel spins, the axle spins too. It reduces friction and converts rotation into forward movement!

⛓ Also a Pulley: The Chain & Gears!

The chain is a pulley system. Power travels from your legs → pedals → chainring → chain → rear cog → rear wheel!

  • Small chainring + big rear cog = more torque, easier to climb
  • Big chainring + small rear cog = more speed on flat roads
"The wheels and the axle help us roll fast! They reduce friction so the bike moves smoothly."
